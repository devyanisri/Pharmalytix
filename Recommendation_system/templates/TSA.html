Time series forecasting is a technique used in statistics and data analysis to predict future values of a series based on historical data points, with the aim of identifying trends, patterns, and seasonal fluctuations.is used in different fields for time-based predictions – like Weather Forecasting models, Stock market predictions, Signal processing, Engineering domain – Control Systems, and Communications Systems.

Assumption of TSA

The key assumption in TSA is stationarity, which means that the statistical properties of a time series remain constant over time and are not affected by the specific time at which they are measured. In other words, the origin of time does not affect the properties of the process under the statistical factor.

Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) Test

Is a commonly used statistical test in time series analysis to check for stationarity. Null Hypothesis (H0): Series is non-stationary Alternate Hypothesis (HA): Series is stationary p-value less than 0.05 Fail to reject (H0) p-value greater or eq 0.05 Accept (H1) N02BA and R06 are non Stationary We used log transformation and differencing for converting to stationary

Arima Model

ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) is a statistical model used to analyze and forecast time series data. It combines autoregressive and moving average components with differencing to handle non-stationary data.
